% Chapter 24 of Genesis
\bookchapter{A Bride for \name{Isaac}}

\bverse Now \Abraham was old, well advanced in age; and the \lord had blessed \Abraham in all things.
\bverse So \Abraham said to the oldest servant of his house, who ruled over all that he had, ``Please, put your hand under my thigh,
\bverse and I will make you swear by the \lord, the God of heaven and the God of the earth, that you will not take a wife for my son from the daughters of the Canaanites, among whom I dwell;
\bverse but you shall go to my country and to my family, and take a wife for my son \name{Isaac}.
\bverse And the servant said to him, ``Perhaps the woman will not be willing to follow me to this land, Must I take your son back to the land from which you came?''
\bverse But \Abraham said to him, ``Beware that you do not take my son back there.
\bverse The \lord God of heaven, who took me from my father's house and from the land of my family, and who spoke to me and swore to me, saying, `To your descendants I give this land,' He will sent His angel before you, and you shall take a wife for my son from there.
\bverse And if the woman is not willing to follow you, then you will be released from this oath; only do not take my son back there.''
\bverse So the servant put his hand under the thigh of \Abraham his master, and swore to him concerning this matter.
\bverse Then the servant took ten of his master's camels and departed, for all his master's goods \textit{were in} his hand. And he arose and went to \location{Mesopotamia}, to the city of \location{Nahor}.
\bverse And he made his camels kneel down outside the city by a well of water at evening time, the time when women go out to draw \textit{water}.
\bverse Then he said, ``O \lord God of my master \Abraham, please give me success this day, and show kindness to my master \Abraham.
\bverse Behold, \here I stand by the well of water, and the daughters of the men of the city are coming out to draw water.
\bverse Now let it be that the young woman to whom I say, `Please let down your pitcher that I may drink,' and she says, `Drink, and I will also give your camels a drink'--\textit{let} her \textit{be the one} You have appointed for Your servant \Isaac. And by this I will know that You have shown kindness to my master.''
\bverse And it happened, before he had finished speaking, that behold, \name{Rebekah}, who was born to \name{Bethuel}, son of \name{Milcah}, the wife of \name{Nahor}, \Abraham's brother, came out with her pitcher on her shoulder.
\bverse Now the young woman \was very beautiful to behold, a virgin; no man had known her. And she went down to the well, filled her pitcher, and came up.
\bverse And the servant ran to meet her and said, ``Please let me drink a little water from your pitcher.''
\bverse So she said, ``Drink, my lord.'' Then she quickly let her pitcher down to her hand, and gave him a drink.
\bverse And when she had finished giving him a drink, she said, ``I will draw \textit{water} for your camels also, until they have finished drinking.''
\bverse Then she quickly emptied her pitcher into the trough, ran back to the well to draw \textit{water}, and drew for all his camels.
\bverse And the man, wondering at her, remained silent so as to know whether the \lord had made his journey prosperous or not.
\bverse Do it was, when the camels had finished drinking, that the man took a golden nose ring weighting half a shekel, and two bracelets for her wrists weighing ten \textit{shekels} of gold,
\bverse and said, ``Whose daughter \are you? Tell me, please, is there room \textit{in} your father's house for us to lodge?''
\bverse So she said to him, ``I \textit{am} the daughter of \name{Bethuel}, \name{Milcah}'s son, whom she bore to \name{Nahor}.''
\bverse Moreover she said to him, ``We have both straw and feed enough, and room to lodge.''
